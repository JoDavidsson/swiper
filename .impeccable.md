## Design Context

### Users
Swiper serves Sweden-first furniture shoppers, especially first-apartment renters and buyers, couples making a joint sofa decision, and browsing-heavy users looking for inspiration. They are usually on mobile, comparing options quickly, and need to feel that the app is fast, trustworthy, and low-friction rather than overwhelming.

### Brand Personality
Assumed from the repo's product positioning and UX goals:
- Calm
- Confident
- Considered
- Modern Scandinavian
- Trustworthy

The interface should feel premium and image-led, but not flashy, precious, or luxury-brand aloof. It should feel like a sharp product tool for real purchase decisions, not a speculative design concept.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-mode-first, mobile-first, image-forward, and tactically minimal. Premium product imagery should do most of the emotional work, while the UI provides structure, speed, and reassurance. Visual references should be closer to Scandinavian commerce/editorial restraint than to neon startup dashboards or gamified dating-app theatrics, even though the core interaction is swipe-based.

Anti-direction assumptions:
- Not loud, playful, or meme-driven
- Not dark-mode-first with glow effects
- Not generic SaaS cards-on-cards dashboards
- Not faux-luxury serif minimalism detached from utility

### Design Principles
1. Let the sofa imagery carry the emotional weight; UI chrome should stay secondary.
2. Make core actions obvious within seconds: like, dismiss, inspect, filter, compare, share.
3. Preserve trust with clear pricing, retailer context, and stable interaction feedback.
4. Keep motion purposeful and tactile; the swipe metaphor should feel responsive, not gimmicky.
5. Prefer Scandinavian restraint over AI-template novelty: fewer decorative surfaces, better hierarchy, stronger product focus.
